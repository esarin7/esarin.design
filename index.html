¡Hola!

My name is Elena Sosa Lerín. I'm a short, curious, and creative communications specialist who writes, shoots, and codes.

Nice seeing you here!

My new website is almost ready, but in the meantime, let me tell you a bit about who I am and what I do.

I'm a proud first generation Canadian from Mexico. I love well-told stories and the Oxford comma. I fervently support intersectionality and human rights. And I'm an enthusiast for (Mexican) street food and baklavas.

Over the past decade I've worked as a media and communications specialist for different international organizations over a wide range of topics like human rights and governance, environmental sustainability, HIV education and prevention, media freedom, and agricultural cooperative entrepreneurship, to name a few. For more details on my professional experiences, take a look at my résumé.

These experiences led me to live (in chronological order) in Thailand, South Korea, Sweden, Malawi, Botswana, South Africa, South Sudan, Kenya, and Colombia. I've also been fortunate to experience the the diversity of Canada, living in places like Winnipeg, Orillia, Halifax, Ottawa, and Calgary, where I'm currently based.

I have a master of arts in international migration and ethnic relations from Malmö University. I studied journalism and political science at both, the University of King’s College and Dalhousie University. Last year, I got a website development certificate from the Southern Alberta Institute of Technology.

I'm passionate about harnessing media and communications as tools to amplify voice, facilitate meaningful participation, and foster social change. I see code as an ideal complement to these tools. It enhances storytelling and makes sense of data in exciting and engaging ways.
